PlayStation 1

The PlayStation 1, released by Sony in 1994, revolutionized the gaming industry with its use of CD-ROMs, which allowed for larger and more complex games compared to cartridge-based systems. Known for its vast library of iconic titles such as "Final Fantasy VII," "Metal Gear Solid," and "Resident Evil," the PS1 established Sony as a dominant force in the gaming market.
